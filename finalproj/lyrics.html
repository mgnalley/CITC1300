Sample Lyrics

Neil Peart's lyrics are known for their intelligence and depth. Rather than focusing on love or heartbreak, Rush explored themes like philosophy, freedom, science fiction, and individualism.

Tom Sawyer: “His mind is not for rent to any god or government.”

The Trees: “There is unrest in the forest, there is trouble with the trees...”

From the hard rock sound of their debut album to the synthesizer-driven experimentation of the 1980s, Rush continuously evolved. Permanent Waves marked a turning point, blending radio-friendly songs with complex musicianship. Later albums like Snakes & Arrows and Clockwork Angels showed the band still pushing creative boundaries well into the 21st century.

Each record feels like a chapter in a novel—cohesive, conceptual, and rich with musical and lyrical themes that resonate across generations. Their work remains essential listening for any serious fan of rock music.

Unlike many rock bands of their era, Rush focused on poetic storytelling and philosophical introspection. Neil Peart, who wrote most of the lyrics, drew from literature, science fiction, and real-world philosophy. His work invites listeners to think deeply about life’s challenges and triumphs.

Songs like “Subdivisions” explore teenage alienation and conformity, while “Freewill” emphasizes personal responsibility and autonomy. Rush's lyrics resonate with fans who value not just rhythm and melody, but meaning.
